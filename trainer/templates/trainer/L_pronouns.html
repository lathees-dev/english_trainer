Rules for Using Pronouns

A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun to avoid repetition and enhance clarity. There are several types of pronouns, each serving a different function in the sentence.

Types of Pronouns:

  • Personal Pronouns: Replace specific nouns referring to people or things.
    • Subject Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they

      • Example: She is going to the store.

    • Object Pronouns: me, him, her, us, them

      • Example: He gave me the book.

  • Possessive Pronouns: Indicate ownership or possession.

    Example: This book is mine.

  • Reflexive Pronouns: Indicate that the subject and the object of the sentence are the same person or thing.

    Example: She taught herself how to play piano.

  • Demonstrative Pronouns: Point out specific things or people.

    Example: This is my favorite book.

  • Interrogative Pronouns: Used to ask questions.

    Example: Who is at the door?

  • Relative Pronouns: Used to connect clauses or sentences and refer to a noun mentioned earlier.

    Example: The woman who called is my aunt.

  • Indefinite Pronouns: Refer to non-specific people or things.

    Example: Everyone is invited to the party.

  • Reciprocal Pronouns: Indicate mutual actions or relationships.

    Example: They help each other with their homework.

Pronoun Agreement:

Pronouns must agree with their antecedents in number (singular/plural), gender, and person.

  • Number:
    • Example: The boy loves his dog (singular).
    • Example: The boys love their dog (plural).
  • Gender:
    • Example: Sarah put on her coat.
    • Example: The dog chased its tail.
  • Person:
    • Example: I went to the store.
    • Example: We are having a meeting.

Clarity of Reference:

Pronouns must clearly refer to their antecedent to avoid ambiguity.

Example: Anna said Sarah was late (instead of "She was late," which could be confusing).

Using Pronouns Correctly:

  • Subject Pronouns: Use subject pronouns for the subject of a sentence.

    Example: He runs every morning.

  • Object Pronouns: Use object pronouns for the object of a verb or preposition.

    Example: Please give it to me.

  • Reflexive Pronouns: Use reflexive pronouns only when the subject and object are the same.

    Example: She taught herself how to dance.

    (Do not use reflexive pronouns unnecessarily: “Please contact me, not myself.”)

  • Demonstrative Pronouns: Match the noun’s proximity and number.
    • Example: This is my house (singular, near).
    • Example: Those are my shoes (plural, far).
  • Possessive Pronouns: Do not use apostrophes with possessive pronouns.

    Example: The dog wagged its tail (not it’s).

  • Indefinite Pronouns: Some are singular and some are plural.
    • Singular: Everyone is invited.
    • Plural: Many are absent.

Correct Usage of "Who" vs. "Whom":

  • Use who as the subject of a sentence or clause.

    Example: Who is coming to the party?

  • Use whom as the object of a verb or preposition.

    Example: To whom did you speak?

Avoid Double Pronouns:

Avoid using two pronouns unnecessarily.

Example: "My brother is coming," not "My brother he is coming."
